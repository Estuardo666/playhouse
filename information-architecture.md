### 4.2. Featured Show: The Trash Talk

**Block Subtitle:** Short Theatre with Environmental Awareness  
**Block Title:** The Trash Talk  

**Synopsis:**
Two very different students receive community service and are sent to clean up a polluted park near the river. Jake, obsessed with the environment, takes the task seriously. Leo, the class joker, would rather make jokes than sort bottles and cans. Under Mr. Torres' supervision, both move from conflict to teamwork.

**Educational Value:**
The play shows how small actions can create real change, promotes environmental awareness, and transforms English learning into a brief, fun, and meaningful theatrical experience.

## 5. Workshops

**Section ID:** `workshops`  
**Subtitle:** English Workshops  
**Title:** Formative Experiences That Develop Voice, Body, Creativity, and Confidence  

**Introductory Text:**
At PlayHouse, workshops use the power of theatre to make English learning a fun, creative, and memorable experience. Through games, improvisation, storytelling, music, and acting, students discover new ways to express themselves while strengthening their communication skills.

---

### 5.1. Introduction to Theatre

**Block Subtitle:** Stage Fundamentals  
**Block Title:** Introduction to Theatre  

**Description:**
An ideal workshop for students to approach English in a playful way using body, voice, and emotions as expressive tools.

**Objectives:**
- Learn theatre fundamentals: voice, body, space, and character
- Foster collaborative work in English
- Develop confidence and oral spontaneity

**Expected Results:**
- Active participation in theatrical dynamics in English
- Initial development of characters and simple scenes
- Greater security in oral expression

---

### 5.2. Storytelling

**Block Subtitle:** Creative Narration  
**Block Title:** Storytelling  

**Description:**
Storytelling strengthens English through emotion and story structure. This workshop helps build characters, plots, and original narratives in English.

**Objectives:**
- Identify beginning, conflict, climax, and resolution
- Create original characters and plots
- Develop oral and performative storytelling skills

**Expected Results:**
- Creation of short stories in English
- Better organization and expression of ideas
- Development of imagination and creativity

---

### 5.3. Improvisational Theatre

**Block Subtitle:** Fluency and Spontaneity  
**Block Title:** Improvisational Theatre  

**Description:**
Improvisation strengthens reaction, active listening, and creativity. In English, it helps reduce fear of mistakes and improves spoken fluency.

**Objectives:**
- Strengthen ability to react in English
- Stimulate creativity and spontaneous use of language
- Build confidence in oral interaction

**Expected Results:**
- Greater fluency in spoken English
- Development of imagination and group collaboration
- Positive attitude toward mistakes and learning

---

### 5.4. Introduction to Musical Theatre

**Block Subtitle:** Voice, Movement, and Acting  
**Block Title:** Introduction to Musical Theatre  

**Description:**
Musical theatre integrates acting, singing, and movement in a multisensory experience ideal for learning English dynamically.

**Objectives:**
- Explore basic techniques of singing, movement, and acting
- Familiarize with simple English repertoire
- Foster group coordination and artistic expression

**Expected Results:**
- Presentation of short musical theatre segments in English
- Improvement in intonation and pronunciation through singing
- Increased self-confidence and teamwork

---

### 5.5. Speaking Clearly

**Block Subtitle:** Pronunciation and Projection  
**Block Title:** Speaking Clearly  

**Description:**
This workshop offers practical tools to improve diction, intonation, and vocal projection when speaking English in public.

**Objectives:**
- Identify pronunciation and clarity obstacles
- Develop techniques for breathing, diction, and projection
- Foster self-confidence when speaking in public

**Expected Results:**
- Greater clarity and precision when speaking English
- More conscious use of breathing and voice
- More confidence in presentations and oral interventions

---

### 5.6. Students on Stage

**Block Subtitle:** Real Stage Experience  
**Block Title:** Students on Stage  

**Description:**
The experience of performing in front of an audience transforms English into a real and memorable experience.

**Objectives:**

## 6. Artistic Residency

**Section ID:** `residency`  
**Subtitle:** Artistic Residency  
**Title:** An Immersive Three-Week Experience That Culminates in a School Presentation  

**Introductory Text:**
Learning a foreign language requires immersive, dynamic, and meaningful experiences. PlayHouse's artistic residency offers students a comprehensive experience where theatre, music, storytelling, and improvisation become real tools for learning English.

**Main Description:**
Over three weeks, students participate in combined workshops in theatre, storytelling, improvisation, musical theatre, and speaking clearly, developing language skills through the creation of a final presentation that takes place at the educational institution.

---

### 6.1. General Objective

**Block Subtitle:** Pedagogical Objective  
**Block Title:** Develop English Communication Competence Through a Stage Experience  

**Text:**
The residency aims to strengthen English communication through an immersive experience in musical theatre and storytelling that culminates in a stage presentation within the school.

---

### 6.2. Specific Objectives

**Block Subtitle:** Process Results  
**Block Title:** Skills Developed During the Residency  

**List:**
- Strengthen pronunciation, fluency, vocabulary, and listening comprehension
- Explore voice, body, improvisation, and character building in English
- Foster creativity and teamwork through scenes and songs
- Produce a final stage piece that strengthens student confidence
- Offer the school a cultural experience that integrates art and English in a single process

---

### 6.3. Expected Results

**Block Subtitle:** Experience Impact  
**Block Title:** What the Residency Leaves in Students and Institution  

**Linguistic Results:**
Students demonstrate improvements in clarity, pronunciation, and fluency in English.

**Artistic Results:**
Participants experience the complete theatrical and musical process, from rehearsals to final presentation.

**Personal Results:**
Confidence is strengthened in public speaking and group collaboration.

**Creative Results:**
Characters, stories, and scenes in English are collectively built.

**Institutional Results:**
The school receives a final stage production that showcases learning and leaves an inspiring experience for its community.
